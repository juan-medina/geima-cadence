# Game Design Document (GDD)
**Project Title:** Geima: Cadence  
**Saga / Franchise Name:** The Geima Saga (Earth + Blood)  
**Genre:** Rhythm Action / Endless Runner / Arcade-RPG  
**Platform:** PC / Console (Gamepad Focused)  

---

## 1. Executive Summary & Core Concept

### The Pitch
> **Geima: Cadence** is a rhythm-action endless runner where **the music is your health, and your heartbeat is your shield**. Run, jump, slash, and block to the beat of a high-energy soundtrack to charge your magical escape core and outrun a ruthless empire.

### The Core Pillars
*   **The World is the Beat:** There is no traditional "rhythm highway." The scrolling physical world *is* your timeline. Obstacles approach you from the right, and you must execute the correct martial maneuver exactly as they reach you on the beat.
*   **High-Stakes Survival:** Your rhythm rating is your health. Every missed beat or mistimed reaction permanently depletes your life bar for that run. There is no healing; to escape with full health, you must perform a flawless rhythmic run.
*   **Agnostic Asset Pipeline:** The game is structured so that level visuals are defined strictly by unlocked "visual skins" (Biomes). This allows the developer to swap, expand, or introduce brand-new parallax backgrounds without altering the underlying rhythm-collision logic.

## 2. Universe & Narrative Arc

### Story Synopsis
You are an enslaved prisoner sprinting for survival through a colossal, shifting scrap-graveyard ruled by the brutal **Iron Empire**—an engineered warrior race of Orcs that overthrew their creators centuries ago. To escape, you must reach an ancient, hidden Monolith. Your only hope is a stolen **Geima Stone** in your pack that absorbs kinetic energy; only by running, jumping, and fighting in perfect rhythmic sync with the machine's pulse can you charge the device to tear open a portal to safety.

The ultimate truth lies at the journey’s end. Players who merely survive will teleport to a peaceful new world to live in quiet freedom. However, those who master every track in absolute rhythmic harmony will overload the Monolith, tearing a rift through time itself. They will wake up in a prehistoric age, eventually becoming the mythical **First Sage** who founded the very Empire that would one day enslave their descendants—trapping them in a perfect, tragic time loop.

### The Backstory
Centuries ago, the **Elders**—a high-magic elven/human ruling council—lived in a golden, peaceful empire. Their utopia was entirely powered by **Geima**, a highly volatile, humming magical crystal harvested from the deepest, most unstable mountain veins. Because the mines were toxic and prone to rhythmic seismic collapses, the Elders used forbidden magic to forge a brand-new, resilient worker race: the **Orcs**. 

The Orcs were sent into the dark, brutal depths of the earth. The harsh, toxic mines and the constant tribal fighting in the dark forged them into a hardened, powerful warrior race—slaves who deeply hated their soft creators.

A member of the Elder Council, bored of a world with no wars, no conflict, and absolute peace, secretly contacted the Orc clans. He gave them weapons, taught them how to forge iron armor, and united them into a single, unstoppable legion. The Orc Legion erupted from the mines, conquered the soft utopia, executed the Elders, and established a brutal, sprawling **Iron Empire**.

You are a descendant of those disgraced Elders, enslaved in a massive **Graveyard Quarry**. This is a colossal, shifting canyon where slaves are forced to sift through the scrap metal and shattered stone of conquered castles to rebuild the Empire's massive wooden siege towers. Deep in a hidden cave within the quarry, your family has spent generations secretly assembling a forbidden magical monolith using scraps of stolen Geima-stone and forgotten Elder alchemy. The game begins as your hideout is breached.

## 4. Gameplay & Controls

### The Kinetic Charger Loop
To power the escape gate (The Monolith) at the end of the run, the player must charge the Geima Stone in their pack. The core absorbs kinetic energy and vibration. The rhythmic, steady impact of your boots hitting the ground at high speeds, the clashing of your sword parries, and the physical force of your shield blocks are what actually **charge the core**.

If you slow down, hesitate, or get hit, the rhythmic charge drops, the energy bleeds out, and you lose health. 

### Controller Mapping & Combat Actions
The player's character is positioned on the left-center of the screen. When an obstacle reaches the player's physical coordinates on a beat, they must press the matching face button.

*   **Y Button (Top) - Jump/Vault:** 
    *   *Usage:* Clears low ground hazards (spikes, pits, rubble).
    *   *Visual:* The character performs a high, athletic leap over the hazard.
*   **X Button (Left) - Shield Block (Sustained):** 
    *   *Usage:* Deflects continuous hazard streams (falling rocks, arrow volleys).
    *   *Visual:* The character raises a stout shield and runs defensively while holding the button down over consecutive beats.
*   **B Button (Right) - Sword Parry:** 
    *   *Usage:* Defeats incoming physical enemies (Orc scouts, attack beasts).
    *   *Visual:* The character swings their blade in a clean, horizontal arc, producing a sharp metallic clashing spark on a successful hit.
*   **A Button (Bottom) - Aether Dash (Magic teleport):** 
    *   *Usage:* Phased escape through vertical energy gates, magic barriers, or heavy sliding iron walls.
    *   *Visual:* The character briefly dissolves into glowing blue energy, surges forward instantly, and reappears.

## 5. Core Mechanics & Systems

### Rhythmic Performance as Health
Instead of separate health and scoring meters, your health bar is your rhythmic execution buffer. **Health is non-recoverable.** To reach the Monolith with a full health bar, you must perform a flawless rhythmic run.

*   **Perfect / Good (Successful Action):** Bypasses the threat safely. Your health remains untouched, and you build your score multiplier.
*   **Bad / Late / Miss (Failed Action):** The character plays a "staggered/hurt" pose, the screen flashes red, the core's energy leaks, and **you permanently lose 1 Heart**. 
*   **Zero HP:** The music abruptly scratches to a stop, the run ends in defeat, and you must restart the track.

### Dynamic Tempo Scaling
The game's running speed is directly tied to the song's tempo (BPM).
*   **Slow Tracks (80-100 BPM):** The environment scrolls gently; the character's running animation bounces lazily to the beat.
*   **Fast Tracks (140-170 BPM):** The background blurs, obstacles rush toward you at high speed, and the running animation is a frantic sprint.
*   **The Sync:** Regardless of the speed, obstacles take exactly 4 full beats to travel from the right edge of the screen to your character's position.

## 6. World Structure & Progression

Rather than static, hard-coded stages, the world is designed around **unlocked environments (Zones/Biomes)**. These environments are mechanically identical but visually distinct, built to adapt to whatever modular pixel art assets (such as forest, desert, or volcanic parallax packs) are acquired during development.

### Zone Playlists & Path Selection
Each Zone features a curated playlist of 6 songs, scaling in difficulty (2 Slow, 2 Medium, 2 Fast). Players choose their own path:

*   **The Horizontal Explorer:** Master all the "Slow" starter tracks across every Zone first to see all the different visual assets.
*   **The Vertical Master:** Stay in a single Zone and practicing until surviving its fastest, most chaotic 170 BPM escape run.

### The Score Chase & Medal System
Completing a song awards an **Escape Medal** based on how many Hearts you have remaining at the finish line:
*   **C-Rank (Survivor):** Finished with 1 Heart.
*   **B-Rank (Infiltrator):** Finished with 2-3 Hearts.
*   **A-Rank (Ghost):** Finished with 4 Hearts.
*   **S-Rank (Legendary):** Finished with a full 5/5 Hearts. Collecting S-Ranks across every song in every zone is required to unlock the **True Ending**.

## 7. Visual Art & Animation Specs

The game utilizes a classic, crisp 2D pixel art style. The characters are built using a modular sprite template (such as the LPC layout format) to support clean animation states.

### Environment & Camera Layout
*   **Viewpoint:** Side-scrolling 2D perspective. The camera is locked on the player, while the background uses a multiple-layer parallax effect (Far, Mid, and Foreground) to create an illusion of infinite depth.

### Character Animation States (Asset Creation Specs)
To bring the runner to life, the player sprite assets must support five core animation states:
1.  **The Running Loop:** A constant, energetic forward sprint. The animation frame rate scales dynamically to bounce in sync with the song's BPM.
2.  **The Braced Block:** A strong, forward-leaning defensive stance where the character holds their shield out to absorb impacts while keeping their running legs moving.
3.  **The Sword Slash:** A rapid, wide sword swing that snaps instantly when the attack button is pressed and transitions smoothly back to running.
4.  **The Jump / Airborne Frame:** A tucked, agile mid-air pose used while the character is physically arching through the air.
5.  **The Hurt / Stagger:** A brief, off-balance animation frame triggered when taking damage, visually signaling a missed beat.
